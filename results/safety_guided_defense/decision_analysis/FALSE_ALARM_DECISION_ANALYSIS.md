# False-alarm decision analysis — what is the correct next experiment?

> **Analysis-only.** No training was run, the frozen Variant D protocol was not changed,
> seed 44 was not run, and no seed-42/seed-43 result files or thesis `.tex` files were
> modified. New derived artifacts live under `results/safety_guided_defense/decision_analysis/`.
> Seed-42 test probabilities were exported with the already-added `export_probability_predictions.py`
> (no retraining); validation probabilities for both seeds were generated by the new
> analysis-only script `scripts/analyze_false_alarm_calibration.py` (reusing the frozen
> attack math; perturbations byte-identical). All numbers are test/validation, n=500/496,
> ε=0.030, window-level, digital-domain, white-box.

**Headline:** Threshold calibration **cannot** fix the false-fall alarms. Under PGD the
walk/run false alarms are **high-confidence** fall predictions — and true falls have *lower*
fall-probability than the false alarms — so no probability threshold beats the current argmax
operating point. The correct next experiment is a **motion-class-aware hard-negative defense
(Option D), piloted on seed 42 only**, not threshold calibration and not more seeds yet.

---

## 1. Current evidence summary (seeds 42 + 43)

Both seeds agree on the frozen Variant D safety-guided defense (test, ε=0.030):

- **PGD fall recall improves over the existing FGSM defense:** 0.444 vs 0.089 (seed 42);
  0.289 vs 0.022 (seed 43).
- **Safety-guided selection beats macro-F1 selection:** 0.444 vs 0.133 (s42); 0.289 vs 0.111 (s43).
- **Clean fall recall preserved:** 1.000 / 1.000.
- **Clean accuracy drops:** 0.970→0.746 (s42); 0.978→0.720 (s43); no degenerate collapse (guard holds).
- **False-fall alarms increase:** PGD false-alarms 54→157 (s42), 35→160 (s43).
- **False alarms are walk/run dominated** in both seeds (≈74–76% of PGD false alarms).

## 3. Motion-bias hypothesis

Evidence: walk+run supply ~74–76% of PGD false alarms in both seeds; the fall-adjacent static
postures (lie down + sit down) supply only ~9–12%. Clean false alarms are far lower
(seed 42 D-safety clean total 38; seed 43 clean total 47) and the attack inflates specifically
the motion classes (run 8→65 seed 42; 9→67 seed 43). The macro-F1 checkpoint shows the same
motion skew at lower magnitude. This points to:

- **(C) motion-class-to-fall bias** as the dominant mechanism — the defense maps high-motion
  windows toward "fall," and the adversary exploits that direction.

Against the alternatives: **(A) fall-adjacent posture confusion** is rejected (lie-down/sit-down
are the *smallest* sources); **(B) broad fall bias** is only partial (the skew is concentrated in
motion classes, not uniform); **(D) threshold/calibration** and **(E) training-objective** are
adjudicated by the probability analysis in §4–§6 below. *Uncertainty:* n=2 seeds; the direction
is consistent and large, but magnitudes vary.

## 4. Probability / logit diagnosis (test, PGD@0.030)

*(derived: `false_alarm_probability_diagnosis.csv`; figure: `figures/fig_fall_prob_distributions.png`)*

Fall-class probability by group (median; n):

| Group | Seed 42 median fall-prob | Seed 43 median fall-prob | fall−true logit margin (median) | max-conf (median) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| true fall | **0.35** (n=45) | **0.27** (n=45) | 0.00 | 0.52 |
| walk → fall FA | 0.85 (n=55) | 0.87 (n=52) | ~2.8 | 0.85–0.87 |
| run → fall FA | 0.89 (n=65) | 0.80 (n=67) | ~2.6–2.9 | 0.80–0.89 |
| other non-fall FA | 0.89 (n=37) | 0.83 (n=41) | ~4–5 | 0.83–0.89 |

**Answers:**
- **Are walk/run false alarms low- or high-confidence? High-confidence.** Median fall-probability
  0.80–0.89 and median max-confidence 0.80–0.89.
- **Barely above the boundary, or strongly classified as fall? Strongly.** The fall logit exceeds
  the true class logit by a large margin (~2.6–2.9 nats) for walk/run false alarms.
- **Does this suggest threshold calibration could help? No — the opposite.** True falls have
  *lower* fall-probability (median 0.27–0.35) than the walk/run false alarms (0.80–0.89). The two
  populations are **inverted** in fall-probability space under attack, so any threshold that
  removes the high-fall-prob false alarms removes the (lower-fall-prob) true falls first.

## 5. Binary fall-alert threshold sweep (validation-selected → test-evaluated)

Validation fall-probabilities were generated for both seeds (no test tuning). Thresholds were
selected on **validation PGD@0.030** by four rules and evaluated on **test PGD@0.030**.
*(derived: `threshold_selection_val_to_test.csv`, `test_threshold_sweep_pgd.csv`;
figure: `figures/fig_threshold_recall_vs_falsealarms.png`)*

Reference argmax operating point (test PGD): seed 42 recall 0.444 / FP 157; seed 43 0.289 / 160.

| Seed | Rule | t* | Test recall | Test FP | vs argmax |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 42 | A max val-F1 | 0.07 | 0.933 | 200 | more recall, **more FP** |
| 42 | B max recall, val-FPR≤0.15 | 0.90 | 0.022 | 63 | **recall collapses** |
| 42 | C min cost (FN×10+FP) | 0.05 | 0.956 | 211 | more recall, **more FP** |
| 42 | D min FP, recall≥FGSM-def | 0.63 | 0.089 | 123 | lower FP but **recall ≈ FGSM defense** |
| 43 | A max val-F1 | 0.15 | 0.778 | 191 | more recall, **more FP** |
| 43 | B max recall, val-FPR≤0.15 | 0.85 | 0.000 | 67 | **recall collapses** |
| 43 | C min cost | 0.09 | 0.844 | 224 | more recall, **more FP** |
| 43 | D min FP, recall≥FGSM-def | 0.73 | 0.022 | 116 | lower FP but **recall ≈ FGSM defense** |

**No rule produces a point that beats argmax on both axes.** Validation-selected thresholds
generalize to test (val and test curves track closely), but the recall/false-alarm frontier
passes essentially **through** the argmax operating point — thresholding only slides along the
same curve. To cut false alarms you must give back roughly all of the PGD-recall gain (rule
B/D → recall ≈ the FGSM defense); to keep recall you pay more false alarms (rule A/C).

## 6. Does thresholding look promising?

**No.** Threshold calibration on the fall-class probability **cannot** reduce the walk/run false
alarms without destroying PGD fall recall, because under attack those false alarms are
high-confidence and sit at *higher* fall-probability than the true falls. The argmax operating
point is already on the achievable recall/FP frontier. → A no-retrain threshold-calibration
experiment is **not** recommended. The weakness is in the trained model's adversarial decision
geometry (a training-objective / representation problem), not in the read-out threshold.

## 7. Candidate next experiment decision

| Option | Verdict | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **A** — continue frozen Variant D seeds 44–46 | Defer | Useful for reliability, but premature: we now understand the mechanism and seeds 42/43 already agree. Confirms a known-imperfect operating point without improving it. |
| **B** — no-retrain threshold calibration | **Rejected** | §4–§6: false alarms are high-confidence and higher fall-prob than true falls; no threshold beats argmax. |
| **C** — revise SafetyScore false-alarm penalty | Low payoff | Only changes checkpoint *selection*; it moves along the same recall/FP frontier (macro-F1 already shows the low-FP/low-recall end). It cannot change the frontier itself. |
| **D** — motion-class-aware hard-negative defense | **Indicated** | Meets the decision rule exactly: walk/run false alarms are high-confidence and thresholding cannot fix them. Targets the observed source (adversarial walk/run → fall) at the training-objective level. Requires retraining. |
| **E** — keep adding seeds without analysis | Not recommended | The robustness gap and the failure mechanism are already characterized; more seeds now add cost without addressing the weakness. |

## 8. Recommended next step

**Design and pilot Option D — a motion-class-aware hard-negative adversarial-training defense —
on seed 42 only.** The diagnosis (high-confidence walk/run → fall under PGD; true falls at lower
fall-probability; no thresholding fix) is the textbook condition for a hard-negative objective
that explicitly penalizes adversarial walk/run windows being classified as fall (e.g., an added
loss term on non-fall→fall transitions, weighted toward the motion classes, and/or mining
adversarial walk/run windows as hard negatives). Validate it on seed 42 against the frozen
Variant D safety-guided result (PGD recall 0.444, FP 157) before any multi-seed.

Keep the **frozen Variant D multi-seed protocol on hold but intact** — it is the confirmed
baseline-to-beat (seeds 42, 43 done), not discarded. Do **not** run seed 44 until the Option-D
pilot either succeeds (then re-decide the multi-seed recipe) or fails (then fall back to
confirming frozen Variant D across seeds with the false-alarm trade-off reported honestly).

*Confidence:* the "thresholding cannot help" conclusion is strong (large, consistent inversion
across both seeds; frontier confirmed). The expected benefit of Option D is a hypothesis, not a
guarantee — it must be measured.
