  \caption[ResNet18 clean-qualified multi-seed fall recall vs.\ perturbation budget]{%
    ResNet18 multi-seed fall recall versus $L_\infty$ perturbation budget
    $\varepsilon$ for the five clean-qualified seeds (42, 43, 44, 46, 48). Each
    curve is the per-$\varepsilon$ mean fall recall across the five seeds for FGSM
    and PGD, with shaded 95\% confidence-interval bands (Student-$t$, $df=4$), on
    the held-out test split ($n=500$ windows, $45$ fall windows); the dashed
    vertical line marks the matched evaluation point $\varepsilon=0.03$.
    Perturbations are digital-domain, processed-tensor perturbations, not
    physical-layer or over-the-air attacks. All five clean-qualified seeds lose
    essentially all fall recall under PGD by $\varepsilon\le0.010$ and reach
    exactly $0.000$ fall recall under PGD at $\varepsilon=0.03$; FGSM is severe but
    reaches exactly $0.000$ at $\varepsilon=0.03$ in only 3/5 seeds. Seeds~45 and~47
    are excluded as non-converged training runs (they appear in the
    convergence-status table only). This is a ResNet18 five-seed clean-qualified
    result over UT-HAR processed-CSI tensors, not a clinical, over-the-air, or
    certified-robustness claim.}
  \label{fig:resnet18-multiseed-fall-recall}
